#ESG Risk Analytics: Climate Risk Monitoring and Site Prioritisation for Business Operations in Australia

Built a synthetic ESG risk analytics project using Python and Power BI to develop risk scores, analyse site-level risk patterns, and create a dashboard for watchlist monitoring and site prioritisation across Australian business sites. The project included data cleaning, feature engineering, composite risk scoring, segmentation analysis, and dashboard design for internal decision support.

##Project Overview
This project was developed as a synthetic ESG risk analytics case study to monitor climate-related operational disruption risk across Australian business sites. Using a site-month panel dataset, I built composite climate risk, vulnerability, business impact, and overall risk scores, then translated the outputs into a Power BI dashboard for trend monitoring, watchlist review, and site prioritisation. The project was designed as an internal decision-support tool for relative risk monitoring rather than a real-world predictive or causal model.

##What I Did
Cleaned and structured a synthetic site-month panel dataset in Python
Engineered features and developed composite climate risk, vulnerability, business impact, and overall risk scores
Analysed site-level risk patterns across states, site types, and reporting periods
Designed a Power BI dashboard for executive monitoring, risk segmentation, and watchlist review

##Tools
Python, Pandas, NumPy, Data Cleaning, Feature Engineering, Risk Scoring, Exploratory Data Analysis, Segmentation Analysis, Data Visualisation, Power BI, Dashboard Design, Risk Monitoring, Business Reporting

##Business Value
The project demonstrates how climate and operational risk indicators can be translated into a practical monitoring and prioritisation framework. Instead of serving as a validated forecasting model, the dashboard was designed to help decision-makers identify elevated-risk sites, review risk concentration across segments, and prioritise monitoring and escalation efforts.

##Note
This is a synthetic, assumption-based project intended for internal relative risk monitoring and prioritisation only. It should not be interpreted as a validated real-world predictive or causal model.
